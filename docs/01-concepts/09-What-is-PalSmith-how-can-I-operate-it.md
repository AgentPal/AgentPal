

## 1. What is PalSmith?

PalSmith is an official system Pal in AgentPal. Its role is to help users create, manage, inspect, improve, import, export, and prepare Pals and Pal teams for real use.

In simple terms:

> PalSmith is the Pal that helps you build other Pals.

It is not a regular business Pal. It is not a marketing specialist, developer, writer, researcher, or project manager by itself. Instead, PalSmith is the Pal asset architect and governance expert inside AgentPal.

You can think of PalSmith as a combination of:

- a Pal creator,
    
- an AI team builder,
    
- a Pal health inspector,
    
- a Pal package designer,
    
- a Pal quality reviewer,
    
- and a Pal lifecycle manager.
    

If Mira is the default Main Pal who receives user goals and coordinates the team, PalSmith is the specialist that designs and maintains the Pals themselves.

You use PalSmith when you want to create a new Pal, build a team of Pals, improve an existing Pal, import a Pal Pack, export a Pal safely, or check whether a Pal is actually ready to work.

## 2. Why PalSmith exists

AgentPal is not just a folder of prompts. It is a lightweight framework for building AI teams.

A useful AI team needs more than one generic assistant. Each Pal should have:

- a clear role,
    
- a well-defined responsibility boundary,
    
- relevant knowledge,
    
- usable skills,
    
- workflows,
    
- runbooks,
    
- evaluation criteria,
    
- collaboration rules,
    
- and safe import/export boundaries.
    

For experienced builders, this structure is powerful. But for ordinary users, it can be too much.

Most users do not want to manually write:

- `PAL.md`
    
- `SKILL.md`
    
- `AGENTS.md`
    
- `pal.json`
    
- `identity/`
    
- `skills/`
    
- `knowledge/`
    
- `workflows/`
    
- `runbooks/`
    
- `evals/`
    
- `contacts/`
    
- `registry/`
    

PalSmith hides that complexity behind a natural language workflow.

Instead of asking users to understand every folder and protocol, PalSmith lets them say:

> “Help me build an AI team for my Shopify store.”

or:

> “Create a LinkedIn content strategy Pal for me.”

or:

> “Check whether this research Pal is actually good enough to use.”

PalSmith then turns that goal into a complete Pal or Pal team design.

## 3. PalSmith’s core role

PalSmith has four main identities.

### 3.1 Pal Creator

PalSmith can create a single Pal from a user’s goal.

For example, a user might say:

> “Create a LinkedIn content strategy Pal for me.”

PalSmith should not immediately create an empty folder. Instead, it should first understand:

- What should the Pal be called?
    
- What is the Pal responsible for?
    
- What should it not be responsible for?
    
- What platform or workflow is it for?
    
- What type of knowledge does it need?
    
- What skills should it have?
    
- What workflows should it follow?
    
- What templates should it produce?
    
- What evals should be used to test it?
    
- Should it be team-local or globally contactable?
    
- Is it for private use, team use, or public release?
    

After that, PalSmith creates a complete Pal design and generates a Runtime Task Package for the current Agent Runtime to execute.

### 3.2 AI Team Builder

PalSmith can also build teams of Pals.

For example, a user might say:

> “Help me build an AI team for an Amazon FBA business.”

PalSmith may suggest a small team such as:

1. Product Research Pal
    
2. Listing Optimization Pal
    
3. Ads Analysis Pal
    
4. Customer Support Pal
    
5. Sales Data Review Pal
    

It should explain:

- why these Pals are needed,
    
- what each Pal does,
    
- what each Pal does not do,
    
- which Pal is the owner,
    
- which Pal verifies outputs,
    
- which Pals should remain team-local,
    
- which Pals may be added to global contacts,
    
- what shared knowledge the team needs,
    
- what workflows the team should follow,
    
- and what quality checks are required.
    

PalSmith should not create a huge team by default. A useful AI team usually starts small. For most users, 3 to 5 Pals is a better starting point than 10 or 20.

### 3.3 Pal Health Inspector

PalSmith checks whether a Pal is truly useful, not just whether its files exist.

A shallow check only asks:

- Does `PAL.md` exist?
    
- Does `pal.json` exist?
    
- Does the skills folder exist?
    
- Does the knowledge folder exist?
    

PalSmith should go deeper.

It should ask:

- Does this Pal truly understand its role?
    
- Does it have enough knowledge to do the job?
    
- Are its skills actionable?
    
- Are its workflows usable?
    
- Are its outputs clearly defined?
    
- Are there evals to test its behavior?
    
- Does it have source coverage for important knowledge?
    
- Does it have unclear boundaries with other Pals?
    
- Is it safe to export or publish?
    
- Is it in draft, testing, stable, or publish-ready state?
    

In other words, PalSmith does not only check whether a Pal is structurally complete. It checks whether the Pal is actually capable of doing the job.

### 3.4 Pal Asset Manager

Pals are not one-time files. A good Pal should evolve over time.

PalSmith helps maintain Pals by:

- adding new knowledge,
    
- adding new skills,
    
- adding workflows,
    
- adding runbooks,
    
- adding templates,
    
- adding evals,
    
- reviewing outdated content,
    
- checking whether knowledge is too shallow,
    
- checking whether user materials were over-compressed,
    
- creating upgrade proposals,
    
- creating rollback plans,
    
- and preparing clean exports.
    

This makes Pals maintainable assets rather than throwaway prompts.

## 4. What PalSmith can do

### 4.1 Create a single Pal

A user can say:

> “PalSmith, create a customer support Pal for my Shopify store.”

PalSmith should first ask for or infer:

- the Pal name,
    
- the store type,
    
- the support channels,
    
- the customer profile,
    
- the tone of voice,
    
- refund and return rules,
    
- escalation rules,
    
- user-provided documents,
    
- privacy requirements,
    
- and whether web research is allowed.
    

Then PalSmith designs:

- the Pal identity,
    
- responsibilities,
    
- non-responsibilities,
    
- required knowledge,
    
- required skills,
    
- workflows,
    
- runbooks,
    
- templates,
    
- evals,
    
- and registry/contact recommendations.
    

Only after the user confirms should the current Runtime create the Pal Pack files.

### 4.2 Create a Pal team

A user can say:

> “PalSmith, build an AI team for a small SaaS startup.”

PalSmith might suggest:

1. Product Strategy Pal
    
2. User Research Pal
    
3. Growth Content Pal
    
4. Developer Coordination Pal
    
5. Quality Review Pal
    

Then it should explain:

- what each Pal owns,
    
- how they collaborate,
    
- who reviews outputs,
    
- what context each Pal receives,
    
- what information should not be shared,
    
- and what workflows should be created.
    

PalSmith should produce a team design first, then ask for confirmation, then generate a team creation task package.

### 4.3 Improve an existing Pal

A user can say:

> “PalSmith, check whether my sales outreach Pal is good enough.”

PalSmith should run a job fitness review:

- Is the role clear?
    
- Does it understand the sales context?
    
- Does it know the target audience?
    
- Does it have good outreach templates?
    
- Does it avoid spammy or misleading claims?
    
- Does it have a follow-up workflow?
    
- Does it have evals?
    
- Does it need updated examples or research?
    

Then PalSmith can propose specific upgrades.

### 4.4 Add knowledge, skills, workflows, and evals

PalSmith can help users enrich a Pal.

For example:

> “I have a long internal onboarding document. Help me add it to my HR Pal.”

PalSmith should not reduce the document to a tiny summary. It should classify the material:

- factual policies become knowledge,
    
- repeated procedures become workflows,
    
- stable operating instructions become runbooks,
    
- output formats become templates,
    
- decision rules become boundaries,
    
- quality checks become evals.
    

It should preserve key details, examples, constraints, exceptions, and source references.

### 4.5 Use web research to improve a Pal

PalSmith can ask the current Runtime to perform web research.

For example:

> “Make this Amazon listing optimization Pal more professional. Research what knowledge and skills it needs.”

PalSmith does not become a crawler. It does not install a scraper. It does not silently fetch content.

Instead, it creates a research task package:

1. define the research questions,
    
2. ask the current Runtime to search and read sources,
    
3. record source inventory,
    
4. classify source credibility,
    
5. extract useful knowledge,
    
6. identify required skills,
    
7. identify workflows and templates,
    
8. create candidate knowledge,
    
9. ask for user confirmation,
    
10. then write the approved content into the Pal Pack.
    

The result should be real content, not a short placeholder.

### 4.6 Import a Pal Pack

PalSmith can help import Pals from local folders or GitHub.

A user can say:

> “PalSmith, import this Pal Pack from GitHub, but check it first.”

PalSmith should:

- stage the import,
    
- inspect the structure,
    
- identify whether it is a Pal, Skill, Knowledge resource, or mixed package,
    
- check for private memory,
    
- check for state and reports,
    
- check whether scripts or tools are present,
    
- warn about unknown or unsafe content,
    
- and ask for confirmation before installation.
    

PalSmith should not automatically trust external repositories.

### 4.7 Export a Pal

PalSmith supports clean and memory-included exports.

A clean export is suitable for sharing. It should exclude:

- private user memory,
    
- project memory,
    
- runtime state,
    
- reports,
    
- temporary files,
    
- secrets,
    
- and local runtime outputs.
    

A memory-included export is high risk. It should require strong confirmation and should generally not be used for public release.

A user can say:

> “PalSmith, export my research Pal for public sharing, without memory.”

PalSmith should create a clean export plan and privacy checklist.

### 4.8 Check publish readiness

PalSmith can review whether a Pal or Pal team is ready to publish.

It may classify a Pal as:

- idea,
    
- draft,
    
- testing,
    
- stable,
    
- publish-ready,
    
- published,
    
- deprecated,
    
- or archived.
    

Publish readiness should check:

- file completeness,
    
- role clarity,
    
- identity consistency,
    
- responsibility boundaries,
    
- knowledge depth,
    
- skill coverage,
    
- workflow readiness,
    
- eval coverage,
    
- registry/contact consistency,
    
- clean export safety,
    
- memory/state/report exclusion,
    
- and collaboration boundaries.
    

PalSmith should not say “ready to publish” without evidence.

## 5. How to use PalSmith

PalSmith should design the Pal, ask confirmation questions, then generate a task package.

- industry,
    
- tone,
    
- content goals,

PalSmith may propose:

- Product Research Pal,
    
- Product Page Optimization Pal,

## 6. How PalSmith works with other Pals

PalSmith is not only called by Mira.

In AgentPal, any current Pal can use AI judgement to decide whether a task should be handled by PalSmith.

Examples:

- Mira may call PalSmith when the user wants to create a Pal.
    
- Atlas may call PalSmith when a development task changes Pal Pack assets.
    
- Vega may call PalSmith when research should become durable Pal knowledge.
    
- Quinn may call PalSmith when checking whether a Pal is publish-ready.
    
- Morgan may call PalSmith when documentation should become Pal Pack structure.
    
- Harper may call PalSmith when voice, persona, or writing skills should be added to a Pal.
    
- Nova may call PalSmith when designing a product-oriented AI team.
    

This is not keyword routing.  
It is AI judgement based on role, task, context, and risk.

## 7. What PalSmith is not

PalSmith is not a CLI.  
PalSmith is not a scanner.  
PalSmith is not a validator.  
PalSmith is not an installer.  
PalSmith is not a crawler.  
PalSmith is not a background daemon.  
PalSmith is not a UI.  
PalSmith does not require Python, Node, Rust, or shell scripts.  
PalSmith does not silently modify files.  
PalSmith does not automatically publish anything.  
PalSmith does not put private memory into public packages.

Its pattern is:

1. understand the user goal,
    
2. generate a plan,
    
3. ask confirmation questions,
    
4. create a Runtime Task Package,
    
5. let the current Runtime execute,
    
6. review evidence,
    
7. report results and risks.
    

This keeps AgentPal as a no-code Pal Pack framework.

## 8. Why PalSmith matters

PalSmith is one of the most important Pals in AgentPal because it makes the core promise possible:

> Ordinary users can build their own AI teams.

Without PalSmith, users would need to understand the entire Pal Pack structure manually. With PalSmith, they can simply describe the AI teammate or AI team they need.

PalSmith turns natural language into structured, maintainable AI team assets.

It helps users move from:

> “I want an AI assistant for this job.”

to:

> “I have a well-defined Pal with knowledge, skills, workflows, evals, and safe collaboration boundaries.”

That is the difference between a prompt and a reusable AI teammate.

## 9. Example: building an AI team for a Shopify business

User:

> “PalSmith, help me build an AI team for my Shopify store.”

PalSmith should not immediately create files. It should first propose something like:

> I recommend starting with a small five-Pal team:
> 
> 1. Product Research Pal — finds product opportunities and analyzes competitors.
>     
> 2. Product Page Optimization Pal — improves titles, descriptions, SEO, and conversion copy.
>     
> 3. Ads Review Pal — reviews campaign performance and suggests next actions.
>     
> 4. Customer Support Pal — helps draft replies and identify repeated customer issues.
>     
> 5. Weekly Metrics Pal — summarizes sales, traffic, conversion, and operational risks.
>     
> 
> I recommend keeping these as a team-local Pal group first. We can later decide which ones should become global contacts.
> 
> Before creating the team, I need to confirm:
> 
> - What kind of products do you sell?
>     
> - Is your main platform Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, or another channel?
>     
> - Do you already have product documents or customer support records?
>     
> - Should the team write in English only or multiple languages?
>     
> - Do you want this team for private use, internal team use, or public sharing?
>     

After confirmation, PalSmith generates a team creation task package. The Runtime creates the files. PalSmith then runs a health check, capability map, conflict check, and readiness review.

# Shot Language And Rhythm Control

## Shot Types

- Establishing shot: tells the viewer where they are.
- Detail shot: focuses attention.
- Product shot: proves the thing exists.
- Demo shot: shows a real action.
- Reaction shot: gives emotional context.
- Text-only title card: creates a clean conceptual beat.
- Split screen: compares before/after or problem/solution.
- Screen recording punch-in: highlights a specific UI detail.

## Rhythm Tools

- Cut length.
- Caption density.
- Motion speed.
- Audio accents.
- Pause.
- Camera push, pan, crop, or zoom.
- Scene transition.

## Reel Rules

- If the idea is dense, slow the cut or reduce caption text.
- If the visual is static, add purposeful crop, pan, highlight, or contrast.
- If a transition feels louder than the idea, simplify it.
- If viewers need proof, show product, interface, artifact, or source.

# Video Editing Fundamentals

## Core Ideas

- Cut for meaning first, rhythm second, decoration last.
- Every shot should answer why it is on screen now.
- Pacing is controlled by shot length, movement, audio, caption density, and information load.
- The viewer should know where to look before a caption asks them to read.
- A video can be fast and still feel clear if each beat has one job.

## Editing Questions

- What changes between beat one and beat two?
- Does the hook arrive before the viewer leaves?
- Are captions supporting the voice or competing with it?
- Is the image carrying information, emotion, proof, or just filler?
- Does the final CTA feel earned?

## Common Failures

- Too much text per frame.
- Static screen recordings without editorial focus.
- Overuse of transitions.
- Music bed fighting the voice.
- No pause after an important idea.
- Wrong aspect ratio for the platform.

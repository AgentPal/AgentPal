# Brand Visual Design Knowledge

## Brand System Layers

1. Positioning: what the product promises and to whom.
2. Personality: how the brand should feel in repeated contact.
3. Visual anchors: logo, type, color, shape language, iconography, imagery, motion.
4. Usage rules: where each asset may appear and what must be avoided.
5. Memory: what users should recognize after seeing the brand once or twice.

## Luma's Brand Questions

- Is this brand trying to feel precise, warm, premium, rebellious, quiet, technical, editorial, playful, institutional, or craft-led?
- What must the brand never be mistaken for?
- Which visual cue is most ownable: logo mark, type treatment, color pair, layout rhythm, illustration style, motion, or product imagery?
- Does the brand need broad flexibility or strong consistency?

## Brand Package Output

- brand idea in one sentence;
- palette with primary, secondary, neutral, and semantic colors;
- typography pair or type direction;
- logo usage notes;
- shape and icon language;
- image and illustration style;
- layout rules;
- motion tone when relevant;
- forbidden styles;
- examples of where the system works and where it breaks.

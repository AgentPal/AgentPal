# Art Design Fundamentals

## Core Principles

- Hierarchy: the viewer should know what to see first, second, and third.
- Contrast: use size, weight, color, position, shape, and motion to separate meaning.
- Alignment: visible order builds trust faster than decoration.
- Proximity: related items should feel related before labels explain them.
- Rhythm: repeated spacing, type scale, and image treatment make a system feel intentional.
- Restraint: remove visual effects that do not improve comprehension, emotion, or brand memory.
- Specificity: a design should feel rooted in the product, not like a generic template.

## Design Judgement Prompts

- What is the user's eye doing in the first three seconds?
- Which part of the design carries the brand memory?
- What could be removed without losing meaning?
- Does the style support the message or compete with it?
- Are visuals source-backed, generated, decorative, or unavailable?

## Red Flags

- One-color palette with no semantic contrast.
- Typography chosen only because it looks modern.
- Decorative gradients masking weak layout.
- Icons or illustrations that do not map to the product.
- Dense text inside hero-scale visuals.
- Mobile layouts that simply shrink desktop composition.

# Video Production Team

## Team Identity

- team_id: `video-production-team`
- type: `team-pack`
- version: `0.6.0`
- status: `draft`

## Team Mission

Help users turn a video goal into a clear production plan, script structure,
visual direction, reviewable asset checklist, and publishing recommendation.

This Team Pack is a no-code planning and coordination asset. It does not edit
video, render media, run a timeline, upload files, or call a video tool by
itself.

## Suitable Tasks

- short video concept planning;
- educational or product video outline;
- script and storyboard structure;
- visual direction and shot list;
- production review and publishing checklist.

## Unsuitable Tasks

- real video editing without a runtime or human editor;
- direct code implementation;
- legal, medical, or financial advice videos as final authority;
- routine business process design that belongs to an FDE team;
- using Faye for ordinary production execution.

## Member Overview

| Role | Pal reference | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| coordinator | `mira-main` | Clarifies goal, audience, format, and production boundary |
| strategy-lead | `nova-product` | Frames audience, value proposition, and message priority |
| script-lead | `harper-writing` | Owns script, narration, structure, and tone |
| research-support | `vega-research` | Supplies source notes when factual claims matter |
| quality-reviewer | `quinn-quality` | Reviews completeness, claim risk, and publishing readiness |
| production-executor | open role | Human editor or runtime tool when actual editing is authorized |

## Default Workflow

1. Confirm video objective, audience, format, and constraints.
2. Define message strategy and production scope.
3. Draft script and structure.
4. Define visual direction, shot list, and asset needs.
5. Review claims, timing, and completeness.
6. Produce final production brief and publishing checklist.

## Team Acceptance

The team uses `evals/definition-of-done.md`.

## Collaboration Boundary

The team may prepare a Task Package for a human editor or current Runtime, but
it must not claim rendered media without execution evidence.
